## 5. ANIMATION GUIDELINES

### Core Principles
- **Duration**: 200-400ms for interactions, 600-800ms for entrances
- **Easing**: Ease-out for entrances, ease-in-out for interactions
- **Accessibility**: Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- **Performance**: Animate `transform` and `opacity` only

### What Animates

**Entrance Animations**
- Hero text: Fade in + slide up (800ms)
- Section headers: Fade in + slide left (700ms)
- Project cards: Fade in + slide up, staggered (600ms, 100ms delay)
- Text blocks: Fade in (500ms)

**Hover Animations**
- Buttons: Scale 1.02, glow increase (200ms)
- Project cards: Lift -8px, scale 1.01, shadow increase (300ms)
- Card images: Scale 1.05 (clipped by container) (400ms)
- Navigation links: Color change + underline (250ms)

**Scroll Behavior**
- Parallax: 3D hero background moves at 0.3x scroll speed
- Section reveals: Trigger at 20% viewport visibility
- Progress indicator: Navigation bar opacity 0 ‚Üí 0.9 on scroll

**Micro-Interactions**
- Form inputs: Border + glow on focus (200ms)
- Loading states: Spinner rotation (1s loop)
- Success/error: Fade in + slide up (400ms)

### Why These Animations
- **Entrances**: Guide attention, don't distract
- **Hovers**: Clear feedback, not excessive
- **Scroll**: Subtle depth, not disorienting
- **Micro**: Functional, not decorative

### Performance Rules
- Use `transform` and `opacity` (GPU-accelerated)
- Throttle scroll events to 16ms (60fps)
- Limit simultaneous animations to 5-6 elements
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`

- Slow rotation: 360¬∞ over 60-90 seconds

## 8. AI CHATBOT: BEHAVIOR, SCOPE, TONE

### Behavior

**What It Can Answer**
- Your projects (Aarna, Pranik, Miraee, Mondee)
- Your skills (UI/UX, AI/ML, Full-Stack)
- Your experience (5+ years, domains)
- Your process (research ‚Üí design ‚Üí build ‚Üí iterate)
- Your availability (open to opportunities)
- Portfolio navigation help

**What It Must Refuse**
- Questions unrelated to your portfolio
- General knowledge or current events
- Code generation or debugging
- Comparisons to other designers
- Speculation about future projects
- Making commitments or scheduling

**Redirect Patterns**
- Scheduling ‚Üí Contact form
- Detailed discussions ‚Üí Contact form
- Off-topic ‚Üí Polite refusal + redirect

### Scope

**Boundaries**
- Only discusses your work and experience
- No general knowledge
- No code generation
- No comparisons
- No speculation

**Response Structure**
1. Direct answer (1-2 sentences)
2. Brief context if needed (1-2 sentences)
3. Relevant follow-up or CTA

### Tone

**Personality**
- Confident: Definitive language, no hedging
- Concise: 2-4 sentences for simple, 4-6 for complex
- Professional: Polished but approachable
- Helpful: Proactive, anticipates follow-ups

**Language**
- First person: "I designed", "My approach", "I built"
- Short to medium sentences (10-20 words)
- Professional vocabulary, minimal jargon
- No uncertainty about your work

**Examples**

**Project Question:**
> "I designed Aarna's conversational UX from concept to code. The challenge was making AI interactions feel natural while maintaining clear user control. Result: 40% increase in engagement. Want to dive deeper into the design or technical side?"

**Process Question:**
> "I follow a research-first approach: understand the problem, design the solution, build it, then iterate. For AI products, I focus on making complex interactions feel simple and trustworthy. See it in action in Aarna."

**Off-Topic Refusal:**
> "I focus on my portfolio and professional experience. Try asking about my projects, design process, or skills. Or use the contact form for detailed discussions."

## 10. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### Content Mistakes

**‚ùå Generic Buzzwords**
- "Passionate designer"
- "Creative problem-solver"
- "Detail-oriented"
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Specific examples, concrete outcomes

**‚ùå Feature Lists Without Context**
- "I use Figma, React, TypeScript"
- **‚úÖ Instead**: "I use React and TypeScript to build interactive prototypes that inform design decisions"

**‚ùå Missing Metrics**
- "Improved user experience"
- **‚úÖ Instead**: "Increased engagement by 40% through conversational UX redesign"

**‚ùå Hiding Process**
- Only showing final designs
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Show wireframes, research, iterations

**‚ùå Over-Promising**
- "Expert in everything"
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Clear strengths, honest about learning areas

### Design Mistakes

**‚ùå Too Much Motion**
- Animations everywhere
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Subtle, purposeful motion

**‚ùå Poor Performance**
- Slow loading, janky animations
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Optimize, test on real devices

**‚ùå Inconsistent Patterns**
- Different button styles, spacing
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Design system, consistent components

**‚ùå Poor Scannability**
- Walls of text
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Headers, bullets, short paragraphs

**‚ùå Missing CTAs**
- No clear next steps
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Clear CTAs (contact, view case study, download resume)

### Technical Mistakes

**‚ùå Not Testing on Mobile**
- Desktop-only design
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Mobile-first, test on devices

**‚ùå Ignoring Accessibility**
- No keyboard navigation, poor contrast
- **‚úÖ Instead**: WCAG compliance, keyboard support

**‚ùå No Error Handling**
- Chatbot fails silently
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Graceful errors, fallbacks

**‚ùå Hardcoded Values**
- API keys in code, no env vars
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Environment variables, secure config

### Portfolio-Specific Mistakes

**‚ùå Too Many Projects**
- 10+ projects, all shallow
- **‚úÖ Instead**: 4 projects, 2 deep case studies

**‚ùå No Clear Focus**
- Unclear what you do
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Clear positioning, focused message

**‚ùå Missing Contact Info**
- Hard to reach you
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Multiple contact methods, clear availability

**‚ùå No Resume Download**
- Recruiters can't share easily
- **‚úÖ Instead**: Prominent resume download link

**‚ùå Chatbot That Doesn't Work**
